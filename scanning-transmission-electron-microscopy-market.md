<p><p>The Scanning Transmission Electron Microscopy (STEM) market encompasses various types, including Electron Energy Loss Spectroscopy (EELS) and Energy-Dispersive X-Ray Spectroscopy (EDX). EELS allows researchers to study the electronic structure and elemental composition by measuring energy losses of electrons, providing insights into material properties. EDX, on the other hand, focuses on elemental analysis by detecting X-rays emitted from a sample, offering valuable data on elemental distribution. Other techniques may include various imaging and analytical methods that enhance material characterization in STEM applications.</p></p>
