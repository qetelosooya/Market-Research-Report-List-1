<p><strong>The Surgical 3D Mobile C-Arm Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Isocentric C-arms</li><li>Non Isocentric C-arms</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Surgical 3D Mobile C-Arm market comprises isocentric and non-isocentric C-arms, differentiated by their design and application. Isocentric C-arms maintain a fixed point around which the imaging system rotates, ensuring consistent imaging quality and versatile positioning, ideal for complex procedures. Non-isocentric C-arms, on the other hand, lack this fixed pivot, offering greater maneuverability but potentially less imaging consistency. Each type serves specific surgical needs, impacting radiology, orthopedics, and interventional procedures based on desired imaging precision and mobility.</p></p>
