<p><p>Linear strain gauges are devices used to measure the deformation or strain of materials under applied stress. They consist of a thin wire or metallic foil arranged in a zigzag pattern, which is attached to the surface of the material being tested. When the material deforms, the strain gauge deforms as well, causing a change in its electrical resistance. This change is measured to determine the amount of strain experienced by the material.</p><p>The Linear Strain Gauges Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.1% during the forecast period. <p><ul><li>Resistive Strain Gauge</li><li>Fiber Optic Strain Gauge</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The linear strain gauges market is primarily divided into two types: resistive strain gauges and fiber optic strain gauges. Resistive strain gauges operate by detecting resistance changes in conductive materials under deformation, making them widely used in various industrial applications due to their simplicity and reliability. In contrast, fiber optic strain gauges utilize light transmission properties in optical fibers to measure strain, offering benefits like immunity to electromagnetic interference and high sensitivity, making them ideal for applications in harsh environments.</p></p>
