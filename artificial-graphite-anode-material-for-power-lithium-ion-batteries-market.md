<p><p>The artificial graphite anode material for lithium-ion batteries is primarily categorized into three types: graphitized coke-based graphite, pitch-based graphite, and others. Graphitized coke-based graphite is derived from petroleum coke and processed to enhance conductivity and stability. Pitch-based graphite, produced from carbon-rich pitches, offers excellent performance but involves complex production. Other materials may include blends or novel composites that aim to improve energy density and cycling stability. Each type plays a crucial role in enhancing battery performance and lifespan.</p></p>
