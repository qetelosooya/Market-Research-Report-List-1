<p><p>Blockchain technology in financial markets can be categorized into three types: public, private, and permissioned blockchains. Public blockchains are decentralized and open to anyone, promoting transparency and security but facing scalability challenges. Private blockchains are controlled by a single entity, offering enhanced privacy and speed, suitable for internal operations. Permissioned blockchains combine features of both, allowing selected participants to access and validate transactions, providing a balance between transparency and control, making them ideal for consortiums and regulated environments.</p></p>
