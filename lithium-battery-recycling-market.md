<p><p>The lithium battery recycling market comprises several types, each with distinct chemistry and recycling processes. LiCoO2 (Lithium Cobalt Oxide) batteries are prevalent in consumer electronics and are known for their high energy density. NMC (Nickel Manganese Cobalt) batteries, used in electric vehicles, balance energy capacity and thermal stability. LiFePO4 (Lithium Iron Phosphate) batteries offer excellent safety and long cycle life, commonly used in power tools and renewable energy storage. Other markets include LFP, NCA, and solid-state batteries, each demanding unique recycling methods.</p></p>
