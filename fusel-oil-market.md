<p><p>The fusel oil market comprises two primary extraction methods: distillation and membrane separation. Distillation involves heating fermented liquids to separate fusel oil based on varying boiling points, yielding a concentrated product. This method is traditional and widely used in the spirits industry. Membrane separation, on the other hand, employs selective permeable membranes to separate fusel oil from fermentation mixtures, offering a more energy-efficient and environmentally friendly alternative. Both methods serve distinct market needs based on cost, efficiency, and desired purity levels.</p></p>
