<p><p>The smoked mackerel market is divided into two primary types: hot-smoked and cold-smoked. Hot-smoked mackerel is cooked at higher temperatures, resulting in a flaky texture and robust flavor, often enjoyed as a ready-to-eat product. Cold-smoked mackerel, on the other hand, is cured and smoked at lower temperatures, preserving a tender, silky texture while imparting a milder taste, typically requiring further cooking before consumption. Both types cater to varying consumer preferences and usage in culinary applications.</p></p>
