<p><p>The automotive glass encapsulation market comprises various materials, primarily PVC, PUR, TPE, and others. PVC (polyvinyl chloride) is known for its durability and cost-effectiveness, often used in budget-friendly applications. PUR (polyurethane) offers superior adhesion and flexibility, making it suitable for high-performance vehicles. TPE (thermoplastic elastomer) combines the properties of rubber and plastic, providing excellent weather resistance and aesthetic appeal. Other materials in this market can include silicone and EPDM, each catering to specific performance and design requirements in automotive applications.</p></p>
