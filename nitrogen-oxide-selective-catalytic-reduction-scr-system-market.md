<p><p>The Nitrogen Oxide Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) system market primarily features two types of reducing agents: ammonia solution and urea. Ammonia solution involves injecting aqueous ammonia directly into the exhaust stream, facilitating the conversion of nitrogen oxides (NOx) into harmless nitrogen and water vapor. Urea, on the other hand, requires the use of a urea-water solution, which decomposes into ammonia when heated, also aiding in NOx reduction. Both methods are essential for meeting stringent emission regulations in various industries.</p></p>
